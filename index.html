About关于

Yuelin Li is an Assistant Professor at Kiang Wu Nursing College of Macau, where his work sits at the meeting point of gerontological nursing and aging science. He studies a deceptively simple question with far-reaching consequences: as people grow older, what determines whether they stay strong, independent, and well — and what can be done to protect that?

His research traces the trajectory of physical frailty across later life, drawing on large international cohorts and rigorous evidence synthesis. Three threads run through it: the predictors and progression of frailty and its link to mortality; the role of mind–body movement, particularly Tai Chi and Qigong, in preserving physical and cognitive function; and how mental illness shapes disability-free life expectancy among older adults. Across this work, he is drawn to questions that are at once methodologically demanding and clinically meaningful — translating what the data show into care that reaches the community.

He earned his PhD in Nursing from Chungnam National University in Korea, then held postdoctoral appointments there and at the University of Pittsburgh before joining Kiang Wu. He is a registered nurse and a certified Tai Chi for Health instructor, and he serves as a reviewer for journals across nursing, geriatrics, and public health.

李岳霖现任澳门镜湖护理学院助理教授，研究位于老年护理与老化科学的交汇处。他关注一个看似简单却影响深远的问题：人在老去的过程中，如何保持强健、独立与安康，又能通过怎样的方式去守护这份能力。

他的研究沿着身体衰弱在晚年的发展轨迹展开，依托大型国际队列与严谨的证据综合，贯穿三条主线：衰弱的预测因素、进展及其与死亡风险的关联；以太极、气功为代表的身心运动在维持身体与认知功能中的作用；以及精神疾病如何影响老年人的无失能余命。

他于韩国忠南大学取得护理学博士，其后先后在忠南大学与美国匠区堡大学从事博士后研究，之后加入镜湖。他是注册护士，也是经认证的太极健康指导员，并担任护理、老年医学与公共卫生领域多本期刊的审稿人。
